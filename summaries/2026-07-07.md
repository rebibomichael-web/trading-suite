1. Title: The Momentum Unwind Resumes: Your Next Move — July 7, 2026

Overview: The Dow briefly hit a new record above 53K before markets turned negative as the AI/momentum unwind resumed, hammering semiconductors, memory names, and AI-power infrastructure stocks despite strong underlying earnings (Micron, Samsung). The committee debated whether this is a healthy rotation/reset (into financials, healthcare, defensives, and "halo" capital-intensive names) or the start of something worse, while still defending long-term AI capex winners like Amazon, Apple, and Nvidia.

- **Joe Terranova**: Says the market's character has changed — a "fight for leadership" with more momentum deleveraging ahead in semis/memory; points to Micron and Samsung's good-earnings-bad-price-action as evidence, and expects a "lower sideways consolidation," calling it "noise, not a peak." Personally sold Generac (calling it a pure momentum trade about to be liquidated by funds) and bought Costco, seeing the valuation reset in Costco/Walmart as complete with pricing-power tailwinds ahead. Likes Travelers, American Express (financials rotation), and Merck (healthcare momentum). Also flagged ADM as a good re-entry on pulled-back agriculture strength. Big reveal: plans to personally buy Nvidia, comparing its current pullback/retracement pattern to Apple's March breakout setup, with risk to the April 7 gap at $178.
- **Stephanie Link**: Pushes back that Micron/Samsung didn't signal weak demand — Micron locked in $100B in contracts through 2027; says semis have simply run too far (now 19% of S&P vs. 3x 2022 levels) and calls for picking spots. Owns and is adding to industrials (Nova, Quanta, Vertiv, GE Vernova) on massive backlog growth (~30% vs. historical 5%), even if they fall another 20-25%, but trimmed Marvell after its huge run. Defends Amazon (added on weakness) citing AWS accelerating to 28-30% growth, ad revenue run-rate of $70B, custom chips worth up to $100B, and cheap 11x EBITDA vs. historical 18x; more skeptical/frustrated with Meta's negative $11B free cash flow despite 31% revenue growth. On Truist (downgraded to Neutral by UBS, PT cut to $55 from $58), says fundamentals remain solid despite the CEO transition uncertainty until September 1. Still buying ServiceNow, calling it "hated" but mission-critical software trading at 23x forward estimates.
- **Rob Sechan**: Calls the pullback a "reset, not a reversal," noting historical AI-related resets averaged 12% drawdowns vs. this steeper move, but still backs the infrastructure buildout (cites Meta's compute-selling economics — $20B incremental revenue potential from $50B spend). Agrees with the Amazon bull case but flags its exposure to a "two-speed economy" hurting the lower-end consumer. Fully endorses ASML (reiterated Top Pick, $2,092 PT at Morgan Stanley) as the AI infrastructure "ultimate bottleneck." Final trade: Microsoft, saying "the worst of the rewriting is behind us" at 21x, cheap relative to 2022.
- **Josh Brown**: Argues the unwind is "behavioral, not fundamental" — cites Todd Sonders' data on $200B in leveraged tech ETFs (13:1 long/short), amplifying both the rally and the unwind. Bullish on Apple (target $400, sees it going to 400) as the most underappreciated Mag7/8 name given iOS 27, Apple Intelligence/Siri overhaul, foldable phone potential, and its position as the AI "toll booth" across 2.5B devices without heavy capex. Likes Service Titan (Top Pick at Piper Sandler, $115 target) on its new "Max" AI tier, projected to reach a $370M run rate. Discusses the "halo trade" (capital-intensive/hard-to-replicate-by-LLM businesses outperforming capital-light software) per a Goldman note, says it "has legs" for years.
- **Best Stocks in the Market (Josh Brown)**: Highlighted best-in-worst-sector picks — Archer Daniels Midland (ADM), on raised FY EPS guidance ($4.15→$4.70), high soybean oil prices, biofuel blending tailwinds, 94 years of uninterrupted dividends, next catalyst earnings in early August; and State Street, on record Q1 (revenue +16% to $3.8B, earnings +40%, record $5.6T AUM, record ETF inflows, becoming the default fund (SPYM) for new Trump accounts), with $159/$150 as key technical levels ahead of July 16 earnings.
- **Options Action (Oliver Renick)**: Notes a large IGV (software ETF) put-spread seller betting the group holds its February lows after a 27% rebound since April; also flags unusually heavy call buying in Broadcom despite its modest 7% YTD move.
- **Michael Santoli (Midday Word)**: Says Nvidia bucked the broader semi weakness today by acting more "defensive," while the S&P has stayed within 3% of highs despite two months of rotation/chop reminiscent of the flat Nov-Feb equal-weight-outperformance period; sees tentative "green shoots" in software (Salesforce, ServiceNow, Microsoft) alongside cybersecurity strength.
- **Final Trades**: Joe Terranova — Nvidia (personal buy); Josh Brown — Toast (breaking above 200-day moving average); Rob Sechan — Microsoft; Stephanie Link — ServiceNow.
