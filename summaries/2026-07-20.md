**Mega Cap Earnings in Focus: Your Next Move — 7/20/26**

**Overview:** With Leslie Picker filling in for Scott Wapner, the Investment Committee debated what the market wants to see from mega-cap earnings this week (Alphabet Wednesday, Amazon next week) — specifically whether capex needs to keep rising, plateau, or show monetization. Panelists also weighed last week's semiconductor/memory sell-off and Friday's Mag 7 dip on open-source AI fears as rotation/liquidity noise rather than a fundamental turn, while flagging a broadening trade underpinned by a still-resilient economy.

- **Rob Sechan:** Says the real question is return on invested capital, not just free cash flow; watching China's open-source models (DeepSeek-style "moments") and cost efficiencies in memory/data centers as swing factors. He's exited Micron (MU) and Caterpillar (CAT, calling it "significantly overvalued"), stuck with Taiwan Semi (TSM), and bought Broadcom (AVGO) "on sale." On Disney (DIS): likes it at 15x earnings if it can grow 8-10% plus dividend, thinks eventual consolidation/separation of streaming, ESPN and parks is coming. Owns Uber (UBER), getting more interested after the pullback from near $100 into the 60s/70s, likes it as a scaling "super app." Cautious on the broader consumer, ties high gas prices (Iran risk) to consumer psyche even as banks show resilience.
- **Steve Weiss:** Argues there's no single answer on capex — Mag 7 needs ROI proof, semis need bigger budgets. Notes China's cheaper chips/memory could hit Micron/SK Hynix. Says the earnings bar "couldn't be higher" this season; advises not to trade around earnings, just trim outsized winners as normal portfolio management. Sold Uber months ago, sees it as "dead money" given EV competition and inability to hit earnings. Owns Charles Schwab (SCHW) despite BMO's downgrade to Neutral. Final Trade: Goldman Sachs (GS) — buy the post-earnings dip after a "phenomenal" quarter.
- **Joe Terranova:** Owns Alphabet (GOOGL, up ~2.5% on chip-efficiency news); says monetization is the key word this earnings season, citing Google Cloud's 63% y/y growth (3rd straight accelerating quarter) and AWS's 28% growth as the proof points that drove May's highs. Flags Apple, Amazon, Alphabet, Nvidia as the four names to watch. On Boeing (BA) and Lockheed Martin (LMT): both "yellow light" momentum positions taken in the last 90 days, not fundamental convictions — Boeing needs a post-earnings (7/28) restart, defense needs a midterm-election budget catalyst. Owns Schwab, wants to see it clear $107.50 toward $110-115 on retail-driven earnings. On Disney: has taken profits on dips, calls current stance "red light," notes activism has been the only catalyst in 5 years. Discussed Micron/Marvell/Corning's 25-40% corrections as parabolic-momentum unwinds, still above key moving averages. Final Trade: Refiners — Valero (VLO), Phillips 66 (PSX), Marathon Petroleum (MPC), citing diesel cracks above $5.
- **Shannon Saccocia:** Expects only incremental capex increases this quarter (big 2026/2027 numbers already telegraphed), watching credit spreads widening on hyperscaler debt and whether free cash flow could turn negative by 2027-28. Sees Friday's sell-off as liquidity/positioning-driven ahead of geopolitical risk, flags August/Jackson Hole/midterms as volatility catalysts. Believes the broadening trade is supported by a genuinely resilient economy (retail sales, jobless claims, Middle East de-escalation helping Japan/Europe), not just an AI pause. Ties Bristol-Myers' Nvidia AI-computing purchase to healthcare's efficiency/speed-to-market theme. Final Trade: Energy — cheap on a relative basis, doesn't require a "higher for longer" thesis.
- **ETF Edge (Contessa Brewer w/ Jon Maier, JPMorgan chief ETF strategist):** Earnings season is running strong with ~88% of S&P reporters beating estimates (many by double digits) and 20%+ y/y EPS growth for a second straight quarter; banks show a resilient consumer and rising capital-markets activity. Maier still favors tech, describing AI as a "five-layer cake" (models, applications, memory, energy, and infrastructure) feeding capex into industrials and energy.
- **Mike Santoli's Midday Word:** Says the market is in "dispersion" mode with no big macro catalyst — consensus view is semis have taken their medicine, Mag 7 looks de-risked into earnings, and "dips are being bought," keeping the S&P stuck riding its 50-day moving average until Wednesday's earnings rush and VIX futures expiration.
- **The Setup:** Weiss previewed GE Vernova (Wednesday, pre-market) — backlog strong, cost overruns the main risk. Joe previewed United Rentals (Wednesday, after the bell) — options market implies a 6.5% move, watching gross margin (~10bp contraction) and rental revenue growth (~7-8%).
