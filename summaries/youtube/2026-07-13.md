### [Elon Just Changed The AI Race Again](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KUAI0pgml24)
**Brighter with Herbert** · Jul 12

Elon Musk's xAI released Grok 4.5, a new 1.5-trillion-parameter model (3x the size of Grok 4.3), built with Cursor and heavily used internally at Tesla and SpaceX for engineering work; a 2-trillion-parameter version finishes training this month and ships next month. Benchmarks show it roughly matching GPT-5.5 and running just behind Fable 5 while beating Anthropic's Opus 4.8, but the standout is efficiency: it uses far fewer tokens per task (~1.7-2 million) versus Claude Code (~79 million) or DeepSeek (~4.6 million), and costs $2-6 per million tokens (currently 50% off, so ~$12 at "normal" pricing) — dramatically cheaper than Claude or even open-source alternatives if pricing holds. Guests Larry Goldberg and Cern Basher framed this as a major shift: Anthropic's rising prices had been pushing corporate users away, and Grok's low cost plus generous usage limits (one user reported using only 1% of a weekly quota in a day of heavy use, versus 100% for Fable 5) could undercut both proprietary and open-source competitors. They cautioned it's unclear whether xAI can sustain this pricing long-term (Anthropic was also cheap "until it wasn't"), but see Grok 4.5 as a serious challenge to Anthropic and OpenAI, especially as its "no-code" natural-language building capability broadens who can use AI at all.
