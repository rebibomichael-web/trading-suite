### [JUST RECORDED: Tesla's Q2 Earnings Call Had One Massive Surprise](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=msF7HKuCHc8)
**Brighter with Herbert** · Jul 23

Tesla's Q2 earnings call focused heavily on the AI/robotics roadmap rather than near-term financials: Model Y is now described as the world's best-selling car, and FSD is such a strong demand driver that many buyers are "buying FSD with a car attached." Robotaxi is expanding (Florida, Texas, Bay Area) and growing over 10% per week in miles driven, while staying cautious to avoid any accidents that could trigger regulatory crackdown. Optimus is being called a potentially bigger product than the cars themselves, but Musk warned its production ramp will be slow and hard since there's no existing supply chain for a humanoid robot — expect a long, flat start to the scaling curve. Musk teased a new "Terafab" chip plant (location/details to be announced separately) and a development fab in Austin combining chip design, testing, and packaging, both aimed at solving AI-chip bottlenecks needed to scale Optimus. He also described "Digital Optimus" (an AI agent that operates computer screens, built with SpaceX) and "megapods" — boxes combining Tesla AI4 and x86 chips deployed at Supercharger sites to use excess power for AI compute. Overall tone: this is a heavy capex year, but Musk called it one of Tesla's best years ever with an even better 2027 expected, driven by autonomy and robotics rather than car sales growth.
