### [SpaceX’s Next Trillion-Dollar Business?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3LqALndboAs)
**Brighter with Herbert** · Jul 14

SpaceX and Tesla investor Larry Goldberg's guest Jeff Lutz argue that Starship's full reusability (booster plus second stage) will crash launch costs to roughly 10% of Falcon 9's, enabling both cheap Starlink bandwidth (10-100x cost drop) and orbital "data centers in the sky," with the next Starship V3 test flight (in about a week) carrying prototype V3 Starlink satellites that already double as compute-ready hardware. They peg SpaceX's lead over rivals (China, Blue Origin) at roughly a decade, note the first full ship recovery is expected within about 6 months and hourly-relaunch cadence roughly two years out, and say even booster-only reuse already makes Falcon launches under half the cost of a full expendable load. On chips, they discuss Elon Musk's planned "Terafab" — a vertically integrated chip fab combining processing, memory, and packaging for both Tesla and SpaceX — with a smaller pilot facility expected in under three years and the full-scale version a couple years after that, using Nvidia chips in the interim and partnering with Intel and ASML. They frame this as removing high supplier margins (memory at 60-80%, GPUs at 50-75%) from the cost stack, giving SpaceX/Tesla a major cost advantage, and dismiss "AI bubble" concerns given the scale of unmet compute demand ahead.
