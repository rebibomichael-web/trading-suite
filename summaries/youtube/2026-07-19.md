### [SpaceX Just Became an AI Landlord. Here's the Math](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uTn9pR9yavE)
**Brighter with Herbert** · Jul 18

SpaceX guest Brian lays out a bullish financial model where SpaceX/xAI's AI data-center leasing business (Colossus 2) ramps sharply: existing deals with Google (~$1B/month, fully ramped by October), Anthropic and Reflection AI (~$1.4B/month), plus an expected one to two new leases (possibly with Google again, more Anthropic capacity, or Microsoft) could push AI-segment revenue toward an $82B annual run rate by Q4 2026 and $90-120B by 2027. He estimates Colossus 2 already has around 440,000 chips working (per Epic AI aerial-photo analysis) and roughly 2 gigawatts of power online now, growing toward 2-4GW by early 2027 via mobile turbine units (Caterpillar/Solar, APR Energy, Ducson), giving SpaceX a unique ability to bring power online fast versus hyperscalers stuck waiting years for energy buildout. Using Amazon-style depreciation accounting (long-lived assets over 25-30 years, chips/GPUs over ~5-6 years), his base case shows Q4 consolidated operating income around $4.4B (bull case $6.8B, bear case $2.3B) and consolidated quarterly revenue near $18.4B, putting SpaceX roughly on par with Tesla's revenue. The bigger long-term thesis is 20+ GW/year of AI compute capacity on Earth plus a path toward 100GW/year in space by 2030-31, though both speakers caution these projections carry wide uncertainty and hinge on continued deal announcements and buildout execution over the next few months.
