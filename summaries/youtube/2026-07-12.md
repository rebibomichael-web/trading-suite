### [Tesla's Optimus Factory Is Bigger Than Anyone Realizes](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CKmuNDRnJuM)
**Brighter with Herbert** · Jul 11

Tesla and SpaceX insiders Herbert (via guest host Sern Basher) and Phil (Rivian/networking background) cover the humanoid robot race: Neo's hands hit 8 m/s finger speed and are on track for 10,000 units/year, while Figure keeps shipping demos, but Tesla's under-the-radar Optimus "Gigex" factory is nearly the size of Giga Texas and aims for 10 million robots/year — at even $25k each that's ~$250B, roughly matching Austin's entire GDP. They flag Grok 4.5's software rewrite potentially halving AI training time (5 weeks to 2.5), Elon's public praise of Anthropic despite the $7.5-40B SpaceX-Anthropic compute deal, and his claim "SpaceX will be worth more than the rest of Earth" if goals are met. Other notes: FSD v14 rolling out in Korea (even on old Hardware 3 cars), Starlink hitting 10 Gbps in Arctic Alaska, and Giga Texas showing heavy Model Y/Cybertruck/Cybercab output. Phil's main thesis: AI compute demand is growing so fast (~350%/year) that only massive new chip "Terafabs" plus space-based "Starmind" solar-powered data centers could ever meet it — traditional power grids can't supply enough energy on Earth.

### [Tom Lee: S&P 8,000. Then a Drop?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jnJ_4BRiBn0)
**Fundstrat** · Jul 11

Tom Lee raised his year-end S&P 500 target to 8,000 (about $400 in 2027 earnings times a 20x multiple), and thinks 2026 will be a fourth straight double-digit-gain year for stocks, with earnings growth still the main driver. But he warns of a rough patch between June and December—driven by a new, less-predictable Fed chair (Kevin Warsh), the unlocking of massive IPO share supply (like SpaceX's, which frees up over $1 trillion by year-end), lingering oil-supply strain from the Iran conflict, and elevated margin debt (up 55% year-over-year, historically a warning sign)—though he expects any correction to be sharp but short-lived ("V-shaped") as long as the economy itself isn't breaking. He remains bullish on energy, small caps, financials, industrials, Mag 7 and software stocks (IGV), sees Microsoft and Meta as undervalued despite recent declines, and still expects Bitcoin above $100,000 and Ethereum above $5,000 by year-end despite crypto's rough 2026. He flagged some skepticism around AI "quality of earnings" (balance-sheet gains, pricing power, capital raises from hyperscalers) but isn't sounding an alarm on the broader AI trade.

### [Would you let a robot build your next home?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VAQgp-IE-MA)
**Fundstrat** · Jul 11

This Fundstrat clip is about how AI-powered robots could soon transform home construction, not just warehouses and factories. The speaker predicts that within a couple of years, robots and "artists" could build elaborate, ornate homes (like a Louvre-style stone mansion) at costs similar to today's regular houses, essentially reviving old-world European architecture that's too expensive to build in the US now. The co-host ties this to Amazon as their top pick for benefiting from the "robotics age," noting Amazon already has about 1 million industrial robots versus roughly 400,000 combined across the rest of the country. The thesis: Amazon's logistics/warehouse expertise and robot fleet could let it expand into home and office construction and delivery (similar to how Sears once sold mail-order homes), potentially doubling its addressable market to include residential and commercial construction.
