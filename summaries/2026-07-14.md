# Cyber Stocks Hit New Record High: How to Trade It — 7/14/26

**Overview:** Goldman Sachs CEO David Solomon told CNBC's David Faber that the AI capital-spending boom is "early innings," not a bubble, even as his firm posted blockbuster earnings and hit a record high. Meanwhile IBM suffered its worst day ever after warning that clients are diverting software spending toward hardware/AI infrastructure and cybersecurity — but the panel noted cyber names like CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Networks, and Fortinet rallied anyway, a sign the market is now judging software stocks individually rather than as one group.

- **David Solomon (Goldman Sachs CEO, interviewed by David Faber):** Net revenues up 39% YoY, EPS up 78% YoY; stock hit a fresh record above $1,121 (+7%+ on the day). Called the AI infrastructure buildout "early innings" of a technology super-cycle, not a bubble — "there will be bumps," but says the underlying tech's productivity gains are real and long-lasting. Said demand for capital/financing exceeds what the firm can supply. Flat-to-down 2% headcount is a Q2 snapshot quirk (2,300 new hires start in Q3), not AI displacement. Called GC succession story (Kathy Rohmler "digging in") "fake news" — she's advising during the search. Declined to confirm timing on Anthropic/OpenAI IPOs but said Goldman is close to both and wants a lead role. On his own tenure/John Waldron succession: "there'll be a transition, but it's not now."
- **IBM meltdown:** Worst day ever for the stock after CEO Arvind Krishna's shareholder letter cited clients shifting spend to hardware/AI infrastructure and being "distracted" by cybersecurity concerns, causing large software deals to slip. Brian Belsky said his firm owns IBM in its value portfolio (bought near 12x, still up 60% despite the drop) and likes that management pre-announced/under-promised. Josh Brown noted the real warning sign came last week — reports that Starbucks is exploring cutting IBM/Microsoft from its tech stack.
- **Joe Terranova:** Framed the IBM warning as a "gut punch" to the software sector broadly but said the market is smart enough now to differentiate — cyber (CrowdStrike, Palo Alto, Fortinet at record/52-week highs, Octa) is thriving on the same call that torched IBM. Said the story is moving from sector-wide narratives to idiosyncratic stock stories (cites Snowflake, Datadog as names still working). Final trade: NVIDIA — bought more last week, would buy more here.
- **Rob Sechan:** Deferred to others on IBM specifics but agreed IT/software-services spending is rotating away from legacy players toward cybersecurity, calling it a difficult trend to reverse. Final trade: Morgan Stanley, citing continued momentum.
- **Josh Brown (Best Stocks in the Market segment):** Highlighted sticking with prior calls rather than chasing new tickers — Travelers (up ~12% since a June 11 call, 50-day support at $309, still a "live" long above $300) and Delta (up ~23% since a December call, rising 50-day near $80 is the line in the sand, sees a path to $100). On CrowdStrike (+10% today, fresh off a 4-for-1 split): called it one of the biggest winners in the S&P's history. Final trade: JPMorgan, "still the best."
- **Brian Belsky:** Discussed broad bank ownership (BofA, JPMorgan, Wells Fargo, Citi, Goldman) built on scale and relationship-driven investment banking strength; expects wealth management to shine in H2 given rising trading volatility; called Citigroup's turnaround under Jane Fraser complete but still sees more value in Wells Fargo. Final trade: Invesco (QQQ owner).
- **Apple debate:** KeyBank downgraded Apple to underweight with a $250 target, citing slowing iPhone builds, weak U.S. upgrades, changing subsidy models, and downside risk to 2027 Mac/iPad/wearables and services estimates as unit growth slows; also flagged Apple's ~35x valuation as too rich if those risks play out. Joe Terranova disagreed, expects a sideways consolidation before Apple's next leg higher, and dismissed the valuation concern, arguing Apple's lock on the global consumer and pricing power (price hikes sticking as pure earnings growth) make the setup durable; noted the Russell 1000 Value index just added 400bps of Apple in its rebalance.
- **Final Trades:** Josh Brown – JPMorgan; Rob Sechan – Morgan Stanley; Brian Belsky – Invesco; Joe Terranova – NVIDIA.
