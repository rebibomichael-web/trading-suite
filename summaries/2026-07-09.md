# The Momentum Reset and How to Trade It — July 9, 2026

**Overview:** The Investment Committee debated whether the wild swings in chip/momentum stocks (Micron, semis) mark a bottom or just more chop, with Josh Brown pointing to $200B in leveraged ETFs distorting price action. Stephanie Link's new NVIDIA buy and a broader discussion of mega-cap capex spending versus who ultimately profits from the AI buildout (Apple emerging as a favorite "winner") dominated the second half.

- **Josh Brown**: Says it's impossible to call a bottom in momentum names — $200B in leveraged single-stock ETFs (~$500B notional) is exaggerating swings in the SMH. Notes zero 52-week highs in SMH today but ~17% of names at 50-day highs; SMH RSI cooled to 49. On mega-caps, argues the market is right to strip the valuation premium since these companies are plowing 100%+ of cash flow into capex — "building phase, not harvesting phase." Believes Apple is the true winner of the AI buildout given its 2.5B-device install base and "bring your own LLM" Siri strategy; featured Citigroup as his Best Stock in the Market, targeting the 110-115 (200-day MA) zone, noting earnings next week and the pending Banamex spinoff. Also touted CrowdStrike (ARR $5.51B, +24% YoY; free cash flow $468M, 34% of revenue; rule-of-40 score of 59), a long-term holding post 4-for-1 split.
- **Jim Lebenthal**: Doubled his Micron position, calling it both a value stock (7x earnings) and a momentum play — argues styles can intersect but investors shouldn't drift from their discipline. Says Micron/SK Hynix/Samsung represent ~15-20% of S&P earnings growth this year; SK Hynix IPO tomorrow reportedly 7x oversubscribed. Pushed back that mega-caps remain buys for anyone with a 3-month+ horizon given capex will eventually convert to positive free cash flow. Final trade: Citigroup, citing upcoming earnings.
- **Jason Snipe**: Debating whether the recent move is a genuine rotation or just rebalancing; likes Micron as both value and momentum. Bullish on Snowflake (up 21% YTD, EPS +68%, revenue +33%, new AWS deal) and ServiceNow (agentic AI push despite stock down ~30% YTD). Sees Apple benefiting from consumer AI without heavy capex spend, more skeptical on enterprise winners (questions if Microsoft can execute; says Zoom "shouldn't exist" given Teams). Favors Goldman Sachs in financials (IB revenue +48% last quarter, stock +20% YTD). Final trade: NVIDIA, citing life-to-date 87% earnings growth.
- **Stephanie Link** (guest appearance): Bought NVIDIA for the first time — calls it "best in breed on sale" at 18x P/E, 15x EBITDA, cheapest since 2019, having underperformed the group 53% YTD/72% over one year. Cites 97% server-GPU market share, proprietary software platform, 130% YoY earnings growth, 85% revenue growth, mid-70s gross margins, and free cash flow doubling to $206B next year. Also holds Marvell (up 200%) and argues buying good fundamentals at a discount always wins over time.
- **Mike Santoli** (market analysis): Says the momentum unwind's "endpoint" is being debated — Bank of America argues semis "corrected on nothing" and should rebound to highs, but Santoli notes giving back 18% after doubling in 3 months isn't necessarily an anomaly. Calls current tape action "benign" and trend-holding, with volatility mostly internal/rotational (SPX zero-sum: NVIDIA/hyperscalers down while broken momentum names bounce).
