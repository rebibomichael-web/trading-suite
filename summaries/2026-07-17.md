The State of the Tech Trade: Your Next Move — July 17, 2026

**Overview:** Chip stocks led a broad tech selloff after China rolled out a powerful new AI model, pushing the SOX into bear-market territory and sparking debate over whether the semiconductor/AI trade is finally topping or just resetting. The committee also dug into big earnings reactions (Netflix's post-earnings plunge, Meta's reported Anthropic compute deal) and rotated capital between overextended AI names and steadier value/income plays.

- **Dominic Chu (guest host)** opened on the semi selloff: Dow ~-0.1%, S&P ~-0.5% below 7,500, Nasdaq ~-1%; momentum ETF on pace for its worst week since April 2025, DRAM ETF's worst week since inception, and the SOX ETF now down 20% from highs (bear market).
- **Steve Weiss** said semis have been making lower highs for a while (technically troubling) and argued the market is pulling forward "peak spending" fears; flagged China's cheaper/more efficient AI models as a real threat plus rising geopolitical/inflation risk from the Iran conflict driving oil higher. He sold most of his **Caterpillar (CAT)** stake, calling its 35x forward multiple "insanity" for a cyclical, capital-intensive name; also trimmed **Micron** after a big gain ("know when to pick up the chips and go home"); still owns **Meta**, **Apple**, **Dick's Sporting Goods (DKS)**, and likes **UnitedHealth's** quarter.
- **Kevin Simpson** cited Bank of America's view that the correction is a "summer reset, not a fundamental reversal" and said this earnings season will separate haves (execution/ROI) from have-nots; **added to NVIDIA** near $200 (down from ~$230), calling it a near-value stock with an $80B buyback and ~0.5% yield; wrote a **covered call on Apple** (350 strike, covering half the position) while remaining bullish on Apple's 2.5B-device AI opportunity; trimmed some **Netflix** Tuesday at $74 ahead of earnings and exited the rest this week on growth/content concerns, though he still likes the long-term thesis; owns **Chevron**, calling it a disciplined capital allocator working around Iran-related shipping risk.
- **Jenny Harrington** argued speculative "AI to infinity and beyond" trades are giving way to valuation discipline, pointing to strength in defensive dividend names (DVY holdings like Verizon, Kinder Morgan, Dominion, General Mills, Target) as evidence of a topping process in semis; questioned stretched analyst targets on **SpaceX** (~$1,200) and **Netflix** (no sell ratings, lowest target $80); added **American Tower (AMT)** to her discipline-growth strategy (4.2% yield, ~14x FFO, Starlink seen as complement not replacement to towers); called Netflix "dead money," saying AI-driven content creation has dramatically raised competitive threats and the stock deserves a discount to the market multiple; likes Meta's Zuckerberg "playing chess" on the compute-leasing move; owns **Marriott** (up 425% since inception, "permanent compounder," 618,000 new rooms in development).
- **Meta/Anthropic compute deal:** CNBC's Pippa Stevens reported Meta is reportedly in talks to lease compute to Anthropic in a deal worth up to $10B over two years (per NYT), which pared Meta's losses on the day; Weiss floated a new "net compute margin" metric for hyperscalers monetizing excess capacity.
- **Netflix chart of the day:** Shares fell as much as ~7.5%, worst day since April 2022, after slowing revenue growth and a weak Q3 outlook (third straight quarter of deceleration); at least 17 firms cut price targets; J.P. Morgan flagged falling US/Canada viewing, Wolf Research cut its 2027 revenue outlook, Bernstein is watching live sports/ads as an offset, and Bank of America said the quarter didn't change the bull or bear case.
- **Malcolm Etheridge (guest)** bought the Netflix dip, arguing the WBD distraction is behind them, pricing power is intact (subscribers up 40-50M alongside price hikes), and the stock is ~45-50% off its highs — a long-term buying opportunity despite the engagement-metric disclosure changes.
- **Calls of the Day:** Marriott reiterated overweight at Morgan Stanley, target raised to $380 (Jenny owns it); Dick's Sporting Goods reiterated top pick at BTIG, $300 target (Steve owns it); Chevron reiterated buy at UBS, $220 target (Kevin owns it).
