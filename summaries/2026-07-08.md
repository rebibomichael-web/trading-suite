# Markets React to the Iran Developments: How to Trade It — July 8, 2026

**Overview:** Stocks sold off (Dow down ~1.5%, near session lows) after President Trump declared the Iran ceasefire over and said the U.S. could strike again, sending oil sharply higher. The Committee spent most of the hour debating whether the sharp "momentum unwind" in AI/semis/power names is nearing a bottom, with Jim Lebenthal doubling down on Micron and Joe Terranova adding to Nvidia into the weakness.

- **Joe Terranova:** Says enough deleveraging has happened for names like KLA, Lam Research, Applied Materials and Micron to be near a bottom, but warns further downside is possible if Middle East tensions drag the broader market down. Tactically added to Nvidia today, calling it a high-conviction trade targeting a move back to $240 — notes NVDA's forward multiple has compressed to 18x (a 7-year low) vs. 23x for the Nasdaq and 20x for the S&P, while it still holds ~97% of the GPU server market. Also highlighted Apple's low 61% analyst buy-rating and modest $318 price target versus 90%+ ratings for most Mag 7 peers.
- **Liz Thomas:** More cautious than Joe — sees more downside in semis; South Korea's Kospi has entered a bear market, a warning sign for the global momentum unwind, though she thinks money is rotating (into China, U.S. healthcare) rather than leaving equities. On financials (the week's best-performing sector, +~4.5%), she argues it's not "peak earnings" for the group — financials are the second-cheapest S&P sector on a P/E basis and are benefiting from a pickup in capital-markets/M&A activity.
- **Jim Lebenthal:** Says technicals "don't look good" but doesn't buy that the AI capex cycle is over — traces the selloff to Meta's comment about excess compute capacity. Doubled his Micron position today, trading at ~6x forward earnings, citing rising estimates, strategic supply agreements, and coming buybacks; admits he may have misread this morning's technical "tell" but is comfortable on fundamentals. Also likes Delta at 10x next year's earnings ahead of Friday's print, citing falling debt and strong demand.
- **Bryn Talkington:** Notes most momentum names have broken below their 20-day average, with Micron approaching the 50-day; calls the memory-chip drawdown "a healthy correction" given explosive earnings power (cites Samsung operating profit +1800% YoY). Defends Apple's premium multiple versus Meta/Microsoft, arguing Apple's consistent, cap-light model and free cash flow (projected 3x Microsoft's) justify it, and pushes back on gross-margin/memory-cost worries as temporary.
- **NATO summit / Iran (President Trump, via live remarks and Megan Casella):** Trump said the Iran ceasefire is over and the U.S. may strike again tonight, calling Iran's military "decimated" (159 ships, hundreds of aircraft destroyed); said preventing an Iranian nuclear weapon matters more than the oil-price reaction. Touted NATO members raising defense spending from 2% to 5% of GDP and $1 trillion in current U.S. defense spending (headed to $1.5 trillion). Later softened tone, saying full-scale war likely won't resume, but also said he's unsure he wants to make a deal with Iran at all ("let's just finish the job").
- **Options Action (Oliver Renick):** Ahead of Delta's Friday earnings, options imply a 6% move vs. a 4% median realized move over the past year; puts outpaced calls 3-to-1, though the single largest trade was a ~$800K bet on 800 January $90 calls, needing an 11% rally to pay off.
- **ETF Edge (Dom Chu with Mike Akins, ETF Action):** Likes software/cloud names as the "SaaS apocalypse" narrative overshoots reality, plus mid/small-cap thematic plays (e.g., DTEC) left behind by mega-cap semis, and emerging markets ex-China as catch-up trades for the second half.
- **Calls of the Day:** SLB initiated Outperform, $62 PT at Wolfe (Bryn: energy pullback broadly, contingent on oil holding above $70); Bernstein favors integrated-oil exposure with Exxon as top pick, Diamondback seen with the most upside (Bryn likes Diamondback's capital discipline) and Devon also named.
- **World Cup broadcast rights:** Netflix, Disney and Google reportedly circling a ~$2B U.S. rights package (per Alex Sherman); Jim calls that price a bargain and expects a bidding war, favoring Disney; Joe argues Netflix should instead pursue NBCUniversal.
