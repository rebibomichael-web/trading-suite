Title: AI's Next Test: Alphabet Earnings — July 22, 2026

Overview: With Leslie Picker filling in for Scott Wapner, the committee focused on Alphabet's after-the-bell earnings as the key test for whether markets will keep tolerating soaring AI capex, alongside a broader debate about extreme momentum-factor volatility reshaping market structure. Financials, cybersecurity, and several earnings-driven stock moves (GE Vernova, UNH, Capital One) rounded out the discussion.

- **Joe Terranova**: Wants to see Alphabet cloud growth beat the 63% consensus and hit a "whisper number" of 70%, plus color on whether internal TPU usage lowers capex needs; says strong results could reignite the AI/momentum trade in memory, semi-equipment names. Flagged Broadcom (currently ~$393, risk near $350) as a key derivative play on Alphabet's TPU relationship. Bought NVIDIA twice (July 7 at $198, again at $207) purely on momentum/technical breakout, targeting $240. Owns GE Vernova (down 7% on disappointing guidance/margins, calls it a small position he's not adding to) and CrowdStrike (sees no reason to reduce cybersecurity exposure despite the OpenAI/Hugging Face "rogue AI" incident; also likes Microsoft's relative software outperformance). On financials, likes CME, CBOE (bought a month ago), Charles Schwab, RJF, T. Rowe Price, AMG, and flagged Virtu Financial (+78% YTD) as the poster child for trading-engagement momentum; downbeat on Capital One (weakest holding in his JOT ETF) and on ratings agencies (S&P Global, Moody's). Also owns Las Vegas Sands, disappointed by the casino pullback, needs positive commentary tonight. Final trade: NVIDIA, target $240.
- **Liz Thomas (Young)**: Views Alphabet's AI-specific ROI as the most important signal, not just aggregate business performance; notes hyperscaler free cash flow is expected to turn negative in 2027, meaning investor patience may need to last until 2028. On momentum, cites Goldman data showing the highest 3-month momentum volatility outside a recession in 45 years ("bucking bronco"), expects it to persist/intensify, and warns against "whale hunting" individual hot stocks — prefers owning the broad index (and puts on it) to ride out the churn. Bullish on cybersecurity broadly (CIBR ETF) as a structural growth theme. Final trade: Equal-weight S&P (RSP) to cut through dispersion and momentum volatility.
- **Jenny Harrington**: Contrasts her old-school, high-FCF investing style (where companies must state explicit ROI targets) with hyperscalers' "hall pass" on AI spend; argues Alphabet's earnings won't set a uniform tone for the Mag7/top 10 given how divergent names like Apple (+20%) and Microsoft (-17%) already are. On Capital One/AmEx, says private-market and alternative-asset managers need to "show me the money," not just benefit from improved sentiment — flagged risk in private equity portfolio company valuations 2-3 years out. Owns United Rentals (up 28% YTD, pricey at ~20x forward earnings but holding as a long-term compounder) and Thermo Fisher (down 10% YTD, nervous given Danaher's read-through, reports tomorrow). Final trade: Ardagh Metal Packaging (reported yesterday, ~8.5% yield).
- **Steve Weiss**: Pushes back on the "where's the ROI" narrative as premature and flawed — argues AI returns will show up embedded in other lines (YouTube, search/Gemini engagement) rather than as a standalone line item, and that heavy spend is partly defensive to avoid ceding ground the way Perplexity has fallen behind. Warns of behavioral risk from momentum volatility: individual investors need "steel rods in their spine" to avoid panic-selling at bottoms/FOMO-buying at tops, while professionals treat volatility as opportunity (cited FTAI Aviation's 12% pop today on a new data-center engine order as an example of trading upside vol). Added to UNH after the post-earnings pop faded (bought yesterday near the pullback from a 52-week high of $461), calling it a re-emerging "permanent compounder" at ~20x earnings. Final trade: reiterated UnitedHealth (UNH).
- **Mackenzie Sigalos (setup segment)**: Framed Alphabet's report as a stress test for the whole hyperscaler AI trade — Mag7 lost $2.3T last month, Alphabet's capex has more than doubled, it's raised $140B+ in debt/equity, free cash flow could turn negative next year; street wants ~60%+ cloud growth, a $460B backlog holding up, and search hitting the $63B target.
- **Mike Santoli (midday notes)**: Market breadth is split roughly 2,800/2,800 advancers/decliners; S&P gains are being carried almost entirely by NVIDIA and Broadcom. Alphabet trades at ~24x earnings — cheap vs. its own recent history but rich vs. Microsoft/Meta. Noted S&P 500 is back at 7,500 despite oil and the 10-year yield both being meaningfully higher than at the start of the month.
- **Emily Wilkins (Capitol Hill)**: New crypto market-structure bill text includes a provision banning federal officials, including the president and VP, from issuing or profiting from crypto; Senate Majority Leader Thune hopes for a vote within weeks; KYC/anti-money-laundering language still being reviewed, with senators like Mark Warner conditioning support on strong safeguards.
- **Oliver Renick (Options Action)**: Ahead of earnings, options imply ~9% move for Texas Instruments (thin volume despite a 65% YTD gain), ~5.5% for Tesla (most popular contract: Friday $380 calls), and an outsized ~11% move for ServiceNow, whose options volume has already doubled today.
- **Final Trades**: Steve Weiss — UnitedHealth (UNH); Jenny Harrington — Ardagh Metal Packaging (AMBP); Liz Thomas — Equal-weight S&P 500 (RSP); Joe Terranova — NVIDIA, target $240.
