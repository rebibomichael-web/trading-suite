# A Critical Week for Stocks: Your Next Move — CNBC Halftime Report, 7/13/26

**Overview:** Stocks kicked off a big earnings week with Apple hitting a fresh all-time high while semiconductors and memory names sold off sharply on renewed rotation and rising rate fears after a hawkish speech from Fed Governor Chris Waller. The panel debated mega-cap value opportunities (Meta, Microsoft, Amazon, Nvidia), bank earnings kicking off tomorrow, and fallout from Apple's lawsuit against OpenAI, Oracle's slide to 52-week lows, and a Wells Fargo call urging Disney to exit streaming.

- **Joe Terranova:** Sees Apple as the "all-star of 2026," has been buying breakouts (last around $310) but is pausing near-term after strong momentum, planning to add more post-earnings; cites Citi's $365 target and a skeptical analyst community (only 61% buy ratings, $320 average target) as underappreciated upside. Views the mega-cap move as sector rotation away from semis/memory. Added JPMorgan and CBOE over the past month but is wary that financial-sector expectations have run up too fast in 30 days. Doesn't see the Apple-OpenAI lawsuit/Johnny Ive defection as an existential threat, just routine IP protection. Final Trade: **the refiners.**
- **Stephanie Link:** Called Nvidia her newest buy — cheapest valuation in ~20 years at 18x forward earnings, ~80% revenue growth, mid-70s gross margins, doubling free cash flow, ~97% GPU market share. Also added to Amazon (best-positioned hyperscaler across ads/compute/retail/AWS); still holds but isn't adding to Meta given weak monetization progress. On banks: expects NII to grow 9%, loan growth 7-8%, IB fees +26%, M&A +9%; flagged Wells Fargo (no longer owned, watching for asset-cap lift) as an add candidate. Final Trade: **Capital One** — trading at 7.5x earnings, sees ~$2.6B in Discover-deal synergies not yet priced in.
- **Jim Lebenthal ("Farmer Jim"):** Argued mega-cap tech (Meta, Microsoft) is now in "value territory" and urged sticking to one's investing style through rotation; added to Micron over three weeks (now a ~3% position), citing the upcoming CHIPS Act anniversary unlocking capital returns, and isn't fazed by Micron's ~6% dip. On Disney, pushed back on Wells Fargo's "exit streaming" call (cut PT to $125 from $146, sees +40% upside) — believes streaming is now a "crown jewel" (~$2B annual profit) and blames geopolitics/Middle East tension and weak studio releases for the stock's struggles instead; Disney is a JOT (Josh's ETF) position bought at $103. Final Trade: **BlackRock**, on NASDAQ price-linked AUM benefits showing in Q2 earnings (reports Wednesday).
- **Sarat Sethi:** Still holds Oracle despite the new 52-week low, citing heavy debt-financed data-center buildout (pressured further by wide spreads on Amazon/SpaceX debt offerings) but sees eventual earnings power. Made two portfolio swaps: bought Ferrari (sold-out two years out despite an EV misstep) and Stryker; sold Zimmer and Zoetis (called Zoetis "a mistake"). Discussed strong bank-earnings setup (24.5% earnings growth estimate) and cited Evercore's $7,750 year-end S&P target and Bank of America's Savita Subramanian on broadening earnings momentum. Final Trade: **Morgan Stanley**, on a wealth-management catalyst.
- **Fed/Macro segment:** Steve Liesman reported a hawkish speech from Fed Governor Chris Waller warning the FOMC "will need to consider tightening monetary policy in the near term" if CPI runs hot, noting nearly 70% of core service categories are up more than 3%; September rate-hike odds rose to 77% (July now 47%) and the 10-year yield ticked up to 4.61%, pressuring long-duration semis (Marvell -8%, Micron -6%, Intel -6.5%).
- **ETF Edge (Dom Chu with Matt Bartolini, State Street):** Small caps are outperforming broadly — all 11 GICS small-cap sectors are beating their large-cap counterparts for the first time in 30 years — driven by strong Q3/Q4 earnings revisions and $9B of inflows over three months (90% of 2026's total small-cap ETF flows); small-cap tech is a standout beneficiary of AI capex.
- **Breaking news:** A Florida federal judge voided the president's IRS tax-settlement deal, calling it collusive; Kenvue shares fell after an appellate court revived Tylenol-autism lawsuits (company maintains no proven link); Musk and Altman traded fresh barbs ("scam Altman" vs. accusations about space-data-center promises) following Apple's trade-secrets lawsuit against OpenAI over its Jony Ive-linked hardware team.
