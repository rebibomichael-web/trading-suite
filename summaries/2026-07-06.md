# The Momentum Rebound: How to Trade It — July 6, 2026

**Overview:** The Investment Committee debated whether the sharp NASDAQ/semiconductor rally is a genuine bounce-back or just a brief reprieve after momentum stocks (Micron, KLA, Marvell) got hammered last week. The group also covered SpaceX's forced inclusion in the NASDAQ 100, chip-equipment price-target hikes, and a batch of individual stock calls (Delta, Netflix, Oracle, Alphabet).

- **Joe Terranova:** Says today's rally is "nothing more than a rebound" that still has to prove itself through Samsung earnings, the SK Hynix IPO (Friday), and next week's ASML/TSMC reports. Warns systematic/quant funds are deleveraging out of tech, which will keep volatility elevated in names like Marvell, Corning, and Micron. Doesn't think the highs are in for semis/memory/momentum this year. Trimmed KLA on valuation (~100% move, 51x forward) but didn't exit. Likes CrowdStrike, Datadog, Twilio, and flags Zoom Video for its Anthropic stake (invested $51M in 2023, now potentially worth ~$5B). Final Trade: **Apple (AAPL)** — about to make a new high.
- **Rob Sechan:** Agrees with Goldman's "buy the dip" framing on momentum, noting 10 double-digit semi corrections over five years have still produced 30%+ annualized returns; maintains a modest semi overweight (~20% vs. 18% benchmark). Owns AMAT, LAM, and KLA; likes AMAT despite a 140% run and 41x forward P/E because estimates keep getting raised. On Netflix, still likes it despite Goldman cutting its target to $110 from $120 — calls it a "growth at a reasonable price" story now trading at 23x, no longer momentum-driven. Final Trade: **Meta Platforms** — 15x earnings, growing 20%, 3 billion active users.
- **Steve Weiss:** More skeptical — says he'd rather buy stocks that were "severely dislocated" (Broadcom, added to Meta) than chase momentum. Sold Micron entirely last Thursday near the open, saying he'd rather be first out than last, even if it means leaving 10-20% on the table; also argues NVIDIA is no longer the multi-year compounder it was three years ago. Final Trade: **Alphabet** — overdue, been under pressure, due for a bounce.
- **Jim Lebenthal:** Initiated Micron at $350 a few weeks ago and is weighing adding more, betting on second-half earnings broadening and continued DRAM price increases (locked in through the next five years, +40%). On Delta (downgraded to Outperform from Strong Buy at Raymond James/Morgan Stanley, targets $104-$115 ahead of Friday's earnings): likes the company long-term but says it's "ahead of itself" near 13x earnings — wait for earnings before adding. Final Trade: **Apollo Global Management (APO)** — was $140 a couple weeks ago, expects it back there soon.
- **Featured segment — SpaceX joins NASDAQ 100:** Leslie Picker reported ~$4.3B in forced index-fund buying expected as SpaceX enters after the close (weighting under 1% initially), noting the move has likely been front-run and real price discovery comes once insider lockups start lifting (possibly next month). Sechan says there's "evangelistic support" for the name given Musk's unreplicable business model but expects some profit-taking; Weiss says the stock can't be valued on fundamentals and is purely a Musk-momentum trade; Lebenthal noted benchmark managers can't afford to be underweight given how costly that was with Tesla in 2023/24.
- **Featured segment — Michael Santoli's Midday Word:** Says the "structural" story (factor rebalancing, index concentration — semis are 18% of the S&P, hyperscalers 16%) is driving conflicting sector moves, e.g., healthcare down 1.4% and staples down 1.8% simply because AI-capex confidence rebounded — calling the overall tape "sideways" and fragile.
- **Featured segment — Contessa Brewer on World Cup betting markets:** President Trump's intervention reversed a red-card suspension on the USA's top scorer ahead of tonight's Belgium match, flipping betting odds toward the U.S.; Kalshi's World Cup market has surpassed $1 billion in volume, Polymarket up 24% month-over-month, and sportsbooks (Caesars, FanDuel) are also seeing record soccer handle, with ~90% of bets backing the USA.
- **Final Trades:** Rob Sechan — Meta Platforms; Steve Weiss — Alphabet; Jim Lebenthal — Apollo Global Management (APO); Joe Terranova — Apple (AAPL).
